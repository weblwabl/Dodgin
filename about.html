About Us


We founded the Dodgin’ 4 Lou Gehrig’s Disease Dodgeball Tournament in 2011 in support of our Meadow Glens Elementary PE teacher’s wife who was diagnosed with ALS in December 2010. ALS, also known as Lou Gehrig’s disease, is a disease that affects the nerve cells in the brain and spinal cord that control voluntary muscle movement. Patients diagnosed with this disease typically have a life expectancy of 3-5 years after diagnosis. At this time there is no cure for Lou Gehrig’s disease. All proceeds from the tournament are donated to the Les Turner ALS Foundation which provides support and patient care to those afflicted with the disease. They are a national leader in research to find a cure and support 90% of the ALS patients in the Chicagoland area. In the past three years, the tournament has raised over $40,000 for the Les Turner ALS Foundation. We thought an annual dodgeball tournament was a great way to get the community involved and try to make a difference!

~ Delaney Gibbons & Taylor Morrissey
